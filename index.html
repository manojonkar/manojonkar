About Manoj Onkar

A Career Built on
Transforming Organisations

Manoj Onkar is the founder of Management Innovations — an organisation-development ecosystem with a singular mission: Reinventing Management and Helping Leaders Build Extraordinary Organisations.

Over 30 years, he and his team have worked across India, Thailand, the Philippines, and more than 10 countries, helping organisations move from intention to genuine, sustained change. He has trained and coached over 50,000 people across 30+ industries, and has personally trained and certified over 500 trainers, coaches and educators.

He is the creator of ODeX (Extraordinary Organizations) and the CFRO Workshop (Creating Future-Ready Organizations) — both grounded in Theory U by Dr. Otto Scharmer of MIT Sloan, competitive strategy, systems thinking, and 30 years of direct implementation experience. His work integrates organisation development, leadership transformation, strategy, talent partnership, and execution support.

Earlier, Manoj served as Master Trainer and Head of International Partnerships at NIS Sparta (Reliance Group) — at one time Asia's largest corporate training organisation — and as National and Global Faculty with Landmark Education (now Landmark Worldwide), the world's largest personal transformation organisation.

He was privileged to deliver customised leadership programmes for the Chief Minister of Gujarat, Shri Narendra Modi (now Prime Minister of India), along with his team of ministers and IAS officers. One of his Thailand-based clients was awarded by their Prime Minister as Best Managed SME following a two-year OD intervention.

Debut Book

Kill BUSYness,
Get High Performance

Publishing 2026

Hidden inside the word "business" is the word BUSY. Most leaders are running BUSY organisations — confusing movement with progress, activity with results, motion with meaning.

Over 30 years of working with CEOs across India, Thailand, the Philippines, and beyond, one pattern appeared almost everywhere: BUSYness was killing their future. Not lack of effort. Not lack of intelligence. The problem was a leadership one — created at the top, normalised in the middle, and suffered at the bottom.

"An organisation that runs on follow-up runs on surveillance, not trust. Leaders deserve language this precise, and a way back this rigorous."

This book introduces the ROAR model — a clear, demanding pathway from BUSYness to High Performance — and a 12-phase implementation blueprint for building an organisation where excellence is the norm.

R
Reflect
Confront the BUSYness you've created — the purpose gap, the strategy void, the competency compromises.
O
Own
Take responsibility without excuses. You are the source of the good, the bad, and the ugly.
A
Assert
Set non-negotiable standards — for yourself first, then for every level of the organisation.
R
Run
Build High Performance that lasts — through 12 phases of world-class implementation.
